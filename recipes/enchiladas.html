Easy Beef Enchiladas

These Easy Beef Enchiladas will be able to feed you and your family for a fraction of the cost of going out to your favorite mexican restaurant.

Ingredients needed:

  • 1 large can of Red Enchilada Sauce.
  • 1 pound of Ground Beef.
  • 16 ounces of Shredded Cheese.
  • 1 cup Vegetable Oil
  • Taco Seasoning
  • Half a pack of Yellow Corn Toritllas
  • 1 cup Water

Cooking Instructions:

  1. First get some sort of deep casserole type dish and lightly grease the bottom and sides with a tablespoon or so of the oil and begin heating your oven to 400 degrees fahrenheit.
  2. Then begin heating a the rest of the oil in the skillet over medium heat.   3. In another skillet in between toritllas, begin browning your pound of ground beef in another skillet. Once the beef is browned, strain out the fat, then return to the frying pan.
  4. Add a package of Taco Seasoning to the beef and a cup of water, then bring to a boil. Once boiling, turn heat to low and simmer for 5-15 minutes depending on the amount of time you have.
  5. By now, you should have all your toritllas lightly fried, and beef browned and seasoned.
  6. Now open your can of enchilada sauce, and line the bottom and sides of the pan with a bit of the enchilada sauce, then prepare your working station where you will be rolling the enchiladas, prepare for a mess.
  7. Begin by taking a fried tortilla, adding a scoop of beef, then rolling it tight, and placing it seam side down onto the enchilada sauce lined casserole dish. Repeat until casserole dish is full.
  8. Put any remaining beef on top of the rolled enchiladas to use the rest of it for a meat sauce in the end.
  9. Spoon over the Enchilada Sauce evenly across all the rolled enchiladas until no sauce remains, and no enchilada remains dry.
  10. Finally, put entire bag of shredded cheese on top of the enchilada casserole you have created, distributed evenly.
  11. Place into 400 degree fahrenheit oven for 20-25 minutes, but keep an eye on it because different casserole dishes and oven combinations cook at different rates of speed. My best advice is to just keep an eyes on it.
  12. Let cool for 5-10 minutes! The more patient you are, the easier it will be to elegantly serve them, but I typicallly devour these immediately myself.
